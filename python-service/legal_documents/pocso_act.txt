
PROTECTION OF CHILDREN FROM SEXUAL OFFENCES ACT 2012 (POCSO)

OBJECTIVE
To protect children from offences of sexual assault, sexual harassment and pornography 
and to establish Special Courts for trial of such offences.

DEFINITION OF CHILD (Section 2(d))
Any person below the age of 18 years.

PENETRATIVE SEXUAL ASSAULT (Section 3)
When a person penetrates his penis, any object or a part of the body into vagina, 
mouth, urethra or anus of a child, or makes the child do so with him or any other person.

PUNISHMENT FOR PENETRATIVE SEXUAL ASSAULT (Section 4)
Minimum 10 years rigorous imprisonment extendable to life imprisonment, and fine.

AGGRAVATED PENETRATIVE SEXUAL ASSAULT (Section 5)
When committed by police officer, armed forces, public servant, manager of child care 
institution, relative of child, gang rape, or causes grievous hurt, pregnancy or 
mental illness to child.

PUNISHMENT FOR AGGRAVATED PENETRATIVE SEXUAL ASSAULT (Section 6)
Minimum 20 years rigorous imprisonment extendable to life imprisonment (remainder 
of natural life), and fine.

SEXUAL ASSAULT (Section 7)
Whoever with sexual intent touches vagina, penis, anus or breast of child or makes 
child touch or does any other act with sexual intent involving physical contact without 
penetration.

PUNISHMENT FOR SEXUAL ASSAULT (Section 8)
Minimum 3 years, extendable to 5 years, and fine.

SEXUAL HARASSMENT (Section 11)
Whoever with sexual intent utters words, makes sounds or gestures, shows body or 
object to child, repeatedly follows child, threatens child for sexual gratification.

PUNISHMENT FOR SEXUAL HARASSMENT (Section 12)
Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine.

CHILD PORNOGRAPHY (Section 13-15)
Using child for pornographic purposes: minimum 5 years. 
Storage of child pornography without reporting: 3-5 years.

MANDATORY REPORTING (Section 19)
Any person who has knowledge that child sexual offence has been committed must 
immediately report to Special Juvenile Police Unit or local police. 
Failure to report is an offence punishable with 6 months imprisonment or fine.

SPECIAL COURTS (Section 28)
State Government to designate Sessions Court as Special Court for trying offences 
under this Act. Trial must be completed within 1 year.

CHILD FRIENDLY PROCEDURES
Child's identity shall not be disclosed. Evidence recorded in child-friendly atmosphere. 
Child not to be detained in police station at night. No aggressive questioning of child.

CHILDLINE
Call 1098 to report child abuse. Available 24 hours, 7 days a week. Free of cost.
