
NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENTS ACT 1881 - CHEQUE BOUNCE PROVISIONS

SECTION 138 - DISHONOUR OF CHEQUE FOR INSUFFICIENCY OF FUNDS
Where any cheque drawn by a person on an account maintained by him with a banker 
for payment of any amount of money to another person from out of that account 
for the discharge of any debt or other liability is returned by the bank unpaid 
either because of the amount of money standing to the credit of that account is 
insufficient to honour the cheque or that it exceeds the arrangement made by 
the drawer of the cheque with his banker, such person shall be deemed to have 
committed an offence.

PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine up to twice the cheque amount, or both.

CONDITIONS FOR OFFENCE UNDER SECTION 138
1. Cheque must be for discharge of legally enforceable debt
2. Cheque presented to bank within 3 months of issue date
3. Bank must return cheque unpaid
4. Payee must send demand notice within 30 days of cheque return memo
5. Drawer must fail to pay within 15 days of receiving notice
6. Complaint must be filed within 30 days of expiry of 15-day notice period

DEMAND NOTICE REQUIREMENTS
Notice must be in writing. Must demand payment of cheque amount. 
Must be sent within 30 days of receiving bank's return memo.
Send by registered post or speed post. Keep postal receipt safely.

SECTION 139 - PRESUMPTION IN FAVOUR OF HOLDER
It shall be presumed unless contrary is proved that the holder received the cheque 
for the discharge of a debt or other liability.

SECTION 140 - DEFENCE NOT ALLOWED
It shall not be a defence that the drawer had no reason to believe that the cheque 
would be dishonoured.

SECTION 141 - OFFENCES BY COMPANIES
If the person committing offence is a company, every person who at the time of 
offence was in charge of and responsible to the company for the conduct of business 
shall be deemed to be guilty of the offence.

SECTION 143 - POWER TO TRY CASES SUMMARILY
All cheque bounce offences shall be tried by Judicial Magistrate First Class.
Cases must be tried summarily for expedient disposal.

SECTION 143A - INTERIM COMPENSATION
Court may direct drawer to pay interim compensation up to 20% of cheque amount 
during pendency of trial.

SECTION 148 - POWER OF APPELLATE COURT
Appellate court may direct appellant to deposit minimum 20% of fine or compensation 
awarded while hearing appeal.

IMPORTANT JUDGMENTS
Dashrath Rupsingh Rathod vs State of Maharashtra (2014): Complaint must be filed 
where the cheque was presented to the bank.
M/s Meters and Instruments vs Kanchan Mehta (2017): Compounding of offence allowed 
even after conviction.
