
RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT 2005 - COMPLETE GUIDE

OBJECTIVE
To set out the practical regime of right to information for citizens to secure access 
to information under the control of public authorities.

WHO CAN APPLY
Any citizen of India can file RTI application. Non-citizens cannot file RTI.

WHAT INFORMATION CAN BE SOUGHT
Any information held by or under the control of any public authority including:
- Documents, records, memos, emails, opinions, advice, press releases
- Contracts, reports, papers, samples, models, data
- Information held in any electronic form
- Information relating to any private body which can be accessed by the public authority

EXEMPTIONS FROM DISCLOSURE (Section 8)
Information cannot be disclosed if it:
- Affects sovereignty and integrity of India
- Has been expressly forbidden by court
- Causes breach of privilege of Parliament
- Relates to commercial confidence or trade secrets
- Is received in confidence from foreign government
- Endangers life or physical safety of any person
- Impedes investigation of crime
- Relates to personal information with no public interest

HOW TO FILE RTI APPLICATION
Step 1: Write application on plain paper in English, Hindi or local language
Step 2: Address to Public Information Officer (PIO) of concerned department
Step 3: Clearly state what information you want
Step 4: Pay Rs 10 fee by postal order, DD, cash or online
Step 5: Submit in person, by post, or online at rtionline.gov.in

TIME LIMITS FOR RESPONSE
Normal cases: 30 days from receipt of application
Life and liberty matters: 48 hours
Information from third party: 40 days
If PIO transfers to another PIO: 5 days extra

FIRST APPEAL
If not satisfied with response or no response received, appeal to First Appellate 
Authority within 30 days. FAA must decide within 30 days (extendable to 45 days).

SECOND APPEAL / COMPLAINT
Second appeal to Central Information Commission or State Information Commission 
within 90 days of FAA order. CIC/SIC has powers to impose penalty.

PENALTY FOR PIO
If PIO refuses to receive application, does not furnish information within time, 
knowingly gives incorrect or incomplete information: Rs 250 per day penalty 
up to maximum Rs 25,000. Also disciplinary action under service rules.

CENTRAL INFORMATION COMMISSION
Handles second appeals for central government departments. Chief Information 
Commissioner and up to 10 Information Commissioners appointed by President.

RTI AND PRIVATE BODIES
RTI applies to private bodies only if they are substantially funded by government 
or if they perform public functions.
