
PROTECTION OF WOMEN FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT 2005

OBJECTIVE
To provide more effective protection of the rights of women guaranteed under the 
Constitution who are victims of violence of any kind occurring within the family.

DEFINITION OF DOMESTIC VIOLENCE (Section 3)
Any act or conduct that harms or injures or endangers the health, safety, life, 
limb or well-being of the aggrieved person including:
1. Physical abuse: bodily pain, harm, injury, danger to life or health
2. Sexual abuse: any conduct of a sexual nature that abuses, humiliates or degrades
3. Verbal and emotional abuse: insults, ridicule, humiliation, name calling
4. Economic abuse: deprivation of financial resources, disposal of household effects
5. Harassment for dowry or other property demands

WHO CAN FILE COMPLAINT
Any woman who is in a domestic relationship with the respondent and alleges that 
the respondent has committed domestic violence against her.

DOMESTIC RELATIONSHIP includes:
- Marriage or relationship in nature of marriage
- Members of family related by consanguinity, marriage or adoption
- Family members living together as a joint family

RELIEF AVAILABLE (Section 18-23)
PROTECTION ORDER: Prohibits respondent from committing any act of domestic violence, 
entering workplace or school of aggrieved person, contacting her.

RESIDENCE ORDER: Ensures aggrieved person right to reside in shared household, 
restrains respondent from dispossessing or disturbing possession of aggrieved person.

MONETARY RELIEF: For expenses incurred and losses suffered including loss of earnings, 
medical expenses, loss due to destruction of property.

CUSTODY ORDER: Temporary custody of children.

COMPENSATION ORDER: Compensation for injuries including mental torture and emotional 
distress caused by acts of domestic violence.

HOW TO FILE COMPLAINT
Step 1: Approach Protection Officer in your district
Step 2: File Domestic Incident Report (DIR)
Step 3: Magistrate Court hearing within 3 days
Step 4: Ex-parte orders can be passed in urgent cases

PROTECTION OFFICERS
Every district has Protection Officers appointed by State Government to assist 
aggrieved persons. Duty to assist in filing complaints, obtaining medical aid, 
getting legal aid, finding shelter homes.

SHELTER HOMES
State government to maintain shelter homes. Aggrieved person can be given shelter 
in these homes.

PENALTY FOR BREACH OF PROTECTION ORDER (Section 31)
Breach of protection order by respondent is punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year 
or fine up to Rs 20,000 or both. Second breach: imprisonment up to 2 years.

HELPLINES
National Domestic Violence Helpline: 181
Police: 112
